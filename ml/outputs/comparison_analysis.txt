

ANALYSIS: Why CNN Outperforms GMM
========================================

CNN achieved 88.4% accuracy vs GMM's 71.4% (+17.0 percentage points).

1. NON-LINEAR DECISION BOUNDARIES: CNNs learn complex, non-linear feature
   interactions through multiple convolutional layers. GMMs assume each genre
   follows a Gaussian distribution, which is a strong (often wrong) assumption.

2. HIERARCHICAL FEATURE LEARNING: The CNN's convolutional layers learn
   hierarchical patterns — first layer detects basic spectral patterns, deeper
   layers combine them into genre-specific signatures.

3. REGULARIZATION: Batch normalization and dropout prevent overfitting,
   while GMMs can overfit to noise in high-dimensional feature spaces.

4. GMM LIMITATIONS: With 50 features, GMMs struggle with the curse of
   dimensionality. The diagonal covariance assumption loses inter-feature
   correlations that the CNN naturally captures.
